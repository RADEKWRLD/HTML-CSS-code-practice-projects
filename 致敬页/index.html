Juice WRLD (December 2, 1998 - December 8, 2019)

In 2015, he independently released his debut mixtape “What Is Love?”.

In 2017, he signed with music label Grade A Productions.

In 2018, signed to New Vision Records , and released his debut studio album Goodbye & Good Riddance, featuring the track “Lucid Dreams”, which gained him attention.

In March 2019, released his second studio album "Death Race for Love".

In May, he won Best New Artist at the 26th Billboard Music Awards.

On December 8, he died at the age of 21 at Chicago Midway International Airport after a sudden illness.

2020, posthumous album "Legends Never Die" is released, winning Favorite Male Rap/Hip-Hop Artist at the 48th Annual National Music Awards the same year.

In 2021, his second posthumous album "Fighting Demons" was released.

2024 present: "The Party Never Ends"
